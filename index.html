E-commerce Web App

Project Type

Functional & Exploratory Testing - Full-stack e-commerce platform

Product Overview

  • What it does: Online marketplace for retail products with user accounts, shopping cart, and payment processing
  • Target Users: E-commerce customers and merchant partners
  • Key Risks: Payment security, data integrity, cross-browser compatibility

Test Strategy

  • Scope & Priorities: Critical payment flows, user authentication, cart management
  • Risk-Based Decisions: Prioritized security and payment functionality over cosmetic elements
  • Out of Scope: Third-party payment gateway internals, legacy feature cleanup

Test Design

  • Test scenarios covering happy path and error conditions
  • Edge cases: Invalid inputs, boundary values, concurrent transactions
  • Exploratory testing charters focused on user workflows

Defect Examples

Bug #1 - Payment Processing Failure: Severity: Critical

Users unable to complete purchases with certain credit card formats. Root cause: Regex validation too strict.

Bug #2 - Cart Item Persistence: Severity: High

Items disappeared from cart on page refresh. Affected localStorage sync.

Outcome & Learnings

  • Prevented critical payment issues before production launch
  • Improved test coverage from 65% to 92% for checkout flow
  • Established risk-based prioritization framework for future releases

Testing Philosophy

I believe that quality is not just about finding bugs‚Äîit's about understanding user needs, mitigating risks, and building confidence in software. My approach focuses on:

  • Risk-Based Testing: Prioritizing testing efforts where they matter most
  • User-Centric Quality: Testing from the perspective of end users
  • Continuous Improvement: Learning from each project and refining processes
  • Clear Communication: Making testing insights accessible to all stakeholders
  • Balanced Automation: Knowing when to automate and when manual testing is more valuable

How I Work

  • Collaboration: I work closely with developers and product managers to understand requirements and align on testing priorities.
  • Handling Ambiguity: When requirements are unclear, I ask clarifying questions and document assumptions to prevent testing gaps.
  • Communication: I provide clear, actionable defect reports and explain testing decisions in business terms.
  • Proactive Quality: I identify potential issues early through exploratory testing and continuous learning.
  • Mentorship: I enjoy helping junior QA engineers develop their skills and understanding of quality principles.
